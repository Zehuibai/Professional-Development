The environmental commitments of LVMH

LVMH sees environmental protection as more than a responsibility: it's an imperative.

The LVMH Environmental Strategy: LIFE 360

The environment is the key to the future of luxury. As an industry leader, LVMH drives competitiveness by continuously paving ambitious paths to environmental excellence by placing the environmental imperatives of biodiversity, climate change, traceability and circularity at the heart of our business strategy. 

 

Do you know the four questions raised by the environmental emergency? 

They are…

-        How do we further reduce the environmental footprint of our activities?

-        How can we better protect biodiversity, a rich source of inspiration for our products?

-        How can the circular economy fuel creativity?

-        How do we integrate environmental traceability into the customer experience?

 

In today’s world, the challenge to protect the planet and biodiversity, as well as combat climate change and rising sea levels is of such crucial importance that it must be addressed effectively and seriously.

 

By striving for better industry-wide standards regarding sustainability, we create a higher baseline for excellence together. From site management to packaging design, the Group is committed to improving its methods every day and creating products in a more sustainable way.

 

Our Track Record

LVMH was the first group among CAC 40 companies to create and develop an environmental department

 

LVMH environmental policy has been a pillar of our growth strategy for over 25 years. LVMH was the first group among CAC 40 companies (Paris stock market index) to create and develop an environmental department in 1992.  Since becoming one of the first major corporations to implement an environmental strategy, we have persistently intensified our efforts to reduce the impact of our business. 

 

LVMH & LIFE 360

The LIFE 360 program’s priorities are to create products that forge a fresh alliance between LVMH and nature

 

The LVMH approach to environmental responsibility is structured by the LIFE (LVMH Initiatives For the Environment) program. LIFE forms the backbone of LVMH environmental policy. The program has structured the Group’s actions around a unified vision, a collective commitment and priorities shared by the Maisons, with a long-term perspective. As a result, the Maisons have been able to effectively integrate environmental considerations into their strategy and managerial processes.

 

Today, the Group relies on the LIFE 360 program, whose priorities are to create products that forge a fresh alliance between LVMH and nature, and which make a positive contribution to biodiversity and to fighting climate change.

 

Our Targets

LIFE 360 sets precise targets for 2023, 2026 and 2030. 

 

To meet these challenges, each of our Maisons will contribute to achieving these objectives by establishing their own action plans. Our objectives are anchored by four strategic priorities that embody a bold new vision of luxury:

 

1. Creative Circularity: We will draw on our creativity to drive environmental excellence in our products and their packaging, through initiatives such as eco-design, recycling unsold products, upcycling (making clothes from existing stocks), or identifying alternative materials. 

  • By 2023: New circular services (repairs, upcycling, etc.)
  • By 2026: Packaging - zero plastic from virgin fossil oil
  • By 2030: 100% of our new products will be from sustainable sources

 

2. Traceability: Being able to trace materials from their origins and through each step of their transformation is essential to ensuring responsible practices. To achieve this, we will pursue our sourcing certification policy and actively engage across every link in our supply chains. 

  • By 2030, 100% of our strategic supply chains will integrate dedicated traceability systems

 

3. Biodiversity: Our activities are intimately linked to nature. They would not be possible without agriculture. We intend to take initiatives to restore what we take from nature.

  • By 2026: 100% of strategic raw materials certified to standards guaranteeing the preservation of ecosystems and water resources
  • By 2030: 5 million hectares (50,000 sq. km.) of flora and fauna habitat restored and/or preserved

 

4. Climate: The aggregate carbon footprint of the LVMH Group amounts to 4.8 million tons of CO2 equivalent. Calculating this carbon footprint has enabled us to identify the most significant sources: raw materials sourcing, packaging and transport of merchandise, both upstream and downstream.

  • By 2026: Reduce greenhouse gases from energy consumption at our sites and stores by 50%. 100% renewable or low-carbon energy at sites and stores
  • By 2030: Reduce and/or avoid by 55% GHG emissions linked to raw materials and transport per unit of added value

 

We are motivated by a strong desire to achieve continuous improvement at every level of our Group. Our unity and determination will enable us to continue to raise the bar and maintain our position as a leading global champion of environmental considerations.
